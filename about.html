About Our Project

What is a meme?

An internet meme is a piece of information - an idea, image, phrase, or short video - that spreads from person to person online. Memes travel through emails, chats, forums, and social media, where they are copied, remixed, and reshared in countless variations.

The term meme was introduced by evolutionary biologist Richard Dawkins in his 1976 book The Selfish Gene. Dawkins described it as a "unit of cultural transmission," comparing the spread of ideas in society to the replication of genes in biology.

Modern internet memes began to emerge in the mid-1990s, when users shared humorous pixelated animations like the Dancing Baby or the Hamster Dance. With the rise of platforms such as YouTube, Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, memes became more diverse and spread faster than ever before.

What are we going to do?

This project explores memes from the perspective of learning English - showing how humor, language play, and cultural references can help expand vocabulary and improve overall language skills.
